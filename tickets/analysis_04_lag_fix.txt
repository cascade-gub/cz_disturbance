THE PROBLEM (visible in the knitted HTML)
-----------------------------------------
In the "band metrics through time" figures (Figure 3 = hbef_w1, and the analogous per-site
figures 7/11/15/19), the bottom "app. lag (d)" panel pins to the ±180 d rails and jumps
near-vertically between +180 and −180 repeatedly. Each jump is ~one period (365 d).

These are 2*pi phase wraps, not real lags. `band_trace` takes a circular mean of `wc$Angle`
at the P band (result on (−pi, pi]) and maps it linearly to days:
    ang <- apply(wc$Angle[bi, ], 2, CMEAN)   # (−pi, pi]
    lag <- ang / (2*pi) * P                   # trapped in (−182.5, 182.5]
Where the true phase sits near +/-pi (antiphase), noise flips it across the branch cut, so
the plotted "lag" flips between +182.5 and −182.5. Those are the SAME physical relationship
(antiphase), so the sawtooth is a rendering artifact of a circular quantity on a linear axis.

Separately, coherence sits ~1 across almost the whole record (top panel). That is
WaveletComp's normalized + smoothed coherence saturating; it is NOT evidence of annual-band
coupling.


WHY — what is actually in the data (verified on hbef_w1__series_daily.csv)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Precip is nearly aseasonal: monthly means 3.2–4.5 mm/d (nearly flat); an annual harmonic
  (cos/sin at 365 d) explains ~0.1% of daily precip variance (R^2 ≈ 0.001). Almost all precip
  variance is at event/synoptic scales (periods of days).
- log(discharge) has a strong annual cycle (annual-harmonic R^2 ≈ 0.22): spring snowmelt high
  (Mar–May), deep summer ET low (Jul–Sep). Driven by temperature / snow / ET, not by a
  seasonal precip cycle. Its annual peak sits ~177 d (≈ half a year) from precip's.
- Consequence: at P ≈ 365 the DRIVER has essentially no signal, so its phase is undefined and
  the phase DIFFERENCE wanders across +/-pi -> the wrapping. The strong, real precip->discharge
  coupling lives at the event / short-period band, which the annual-band read never captures.

So the ±180 sawtooth AND the ~1 coherence are both degeneracies of reading a nearly-aseasonal
driver at the annual band — not measurements. The fix is to stop presenting them as
measurements and to point the reader to where the coupling actually is.
