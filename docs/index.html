Disturbance–response synchrony

Comparing two methods for quantifying driver–response synchrony — peak/moments vs Morlet wavelet — on an idealised system and on real watersheds.

nic · nic@cascadeclimate.org

Idealised — synthetic data (theory & expectations)

  • Data generator How the three series are built (log-linear response, morph mechanism, per-scenario disturbance).
  • Analysis I — central tendency & peak synchrony Log-space moments and peak-derived synchrony, pre vs post.
  • Analysis II — wavelet synchrony Morlet coherence/cross-power at the P≈500 band, plus the transition Doppler chirp.

Real-data — MacroSheds watersheds (the same methods, stress-tested)

  • Prep Carves five screened MacroSheds disturbances into the same climate(precip)/response(discharge)/phase shape the analyses read.
  • EDA — disturbance-site time series Daily discharge & precipitation, grab-sample NO3 & conductance across the five MacroSheds disturbance sites: coverage, within-site stacks, and z-scored synchrony.
  • Analysis III — central tendency & peak synchrony Analysis I's peak/moments method on real precip→discharge across five disturbances — where it frays off the idealised bench.
  • Analysis IV — wavelet synchrony Analysis II's Morlet method on the same five sites at the annual band — coherence saturates, cross-power/amplitude carry the story.
